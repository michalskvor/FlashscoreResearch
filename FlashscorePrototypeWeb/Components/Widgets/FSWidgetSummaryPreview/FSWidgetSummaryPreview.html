This is headline of article

While two recent defeats against Nice and Angers had a damaging impact on their Ligue 1 title aspirations, second-placed Monaco did show a good response in their two outings since, following a UEFA Champions League win over Bologna with a 3-1 success at Strasbourg. Those two aforementioned defeats have seen Adi Hütter's men fall six points behind leaders PSG, but they could claw some of that back - at least temporarily - here against Brest. Monaco's win over Strasbourg came thanks to three quick-fire goals in the final 11 minutes of regular time. That's been a theme of their performances this season, as not only did they also score in this period against Bologna, but seven of their 18 league strikes this season have arrived in the final 15 minutes of matches - no side has scored more such goals in the division.
